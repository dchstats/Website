Welcome to Dudhichua

The Mega Opencast Coal Mine of NCL

Get Started

Dudhichua Opencast Project is one of the Largest Opencast Project of Northern Coalfields Limited, (A Subsidiary of Coal India Limited) .

It is an ongoing Project with a sanctioned capacity of 20 MTY (Normative Production) & 25 MTY (Peak Production).

  • Dudhichua Opencast Project is located on the east of Jayant Opencast Project and on the west of Khadia Opencast Project of Singrauli Coalfield. The Inter State Boundary of Madhya Pradesh (M.P.) & Uttar Pradesh (U.P.) passes through the mine Leasehold Area so this Project is partly in the Singrauli district in M.P. and partly in the Sonebhadra district in U.P. Although the mine area for the Expansion from 10 to 20 MTY is entirely in Singrauli District of Madhya Pradesh.
  • Geographic Coordinates of the Expansion area are bounded by Longitude 82° 41`3.27``E to 82° 40`2.3`` E and latitude 24°9`12.23``N to 24°9`20.28``N, whereas Southern latitude of lease hold boundary is 24°7`19.83``N to 24°10`56.40``N. The Area is covered in Survey of India Topo Sheet no. 63L/12 (RF 1:50000) and special Toposheet no. 6 to 9.
  • The Dudhichua OCP stands out on a plateau above plains on its south – east part of Singrauli Coalfield. The average elevation at the foot of the plateau is 325 m above Mean sea Level. The highest elevation reaches about 504 m. The plateau is defined by a steep escarpment (facing SW), rising from the elevation of 320-430 m. The most important stream in around is baliaNalla. The drainage of the area is controlled by seasonal streams which discharge into BalliaNalla, which ultimately drains into Govind Ballabh Pant Sagar, the largest man made water Reservoir of India. Similarly, towards north, the drainage is through seasonal streams which ultimately join BijulNalla.

Initially Feasibility study of Singrauli Coalfield was prepared by CMPDI in June, 1981 in collaboration with erstwhile SOVIET Experts identified Dudhichua OCP with a rated capacity of 10 MTPA. It was then decided on 11.01.82 in the IMG meeting that the project will be developed in two stages i.e. Stage – I for 5 MTPA and ultimate stage of 10 MTPA.
